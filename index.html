Recycle Right: A Campaign for Change

Turning Awareness into Action

The Problem

Only 21% of residential recyclables are being recycled in the U.S., with 76% lost to household trash despite 73% of households having access to recycling services.

Key Stats:
  • Only 21% of recyclables are captured
  • 43% of households participate in recycling
  • Most recyclable materials (cardboard, cans, bottles) are under-recycled

Why Recycling Matters

Recycling can reduce emissions by up to 6.02 gigatons by 2050 — the equivalent of removing 1 billion cars for a year.

It also contributes economically: 681,000 jobs, $37.8B in wages, and $5.5B in tax revenue (2020 REI Report).

Our Campaign Strategy

  • Website: Central hub for education and statistics
  • News Article: Credible reporting for older audiences
  • Instagram: Visual campaigns like #RecycleRight & #EcoYouthChallenge
  • YouTube Shorts: Short videos for Gen Z reach

Who We're Targeting

Urban Young Adults (18–35): Tech-savvy, eco-conscious, socially engaged

Students via Schools: Learn quickly, create household ripple effects, schools generate significant waste
